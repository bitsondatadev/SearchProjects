<!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="FeatureContent" --> <p>The following courses are normally approved by the graduate committee:</p> <p>Purdue <a href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/academic_programs/courses">CS courses</a> at the 500 level or above, except CS 501. (CS 535 is approved when taught by a faculty member whose primary appointment is in this department.)</p> <p>Other Purdue courses:</p> <blockquote> <p>BIOL 51700: Molecular Biology: Proteins -- if taken for the Computational Life Sciences concentration </p> <p>BIOL 54100: Molecular Genetics of Bacteria -- if taken for the<br /> Computational Life Sciences concentration</p> </blockquote> <ul class="close_list"> <li>ECE 565: Computer Architecture</li> <li>ECE 570: Programming Languages for Artificial Intelligence (IE 547)</li> <li>ECE 600: Random Variables and Signals</li> <li>ECE 661: Image Processing</li> <li> ECE 695B: Fault-Tolerant Computer System Design</li> </ul> <ul class="close_list"> <li>IE 535: Linear Programming</li> <li>IE 547: Programming Languages for Artificial Intelligence (ECE 570)</li> <li>IE 580: Systems Simulation </li> </ul> <ul class="close_list"> <li> MA 51100:&nbsp;&nbsp; Linear Algebra with Applications -- if taken at Purdue as a prerequisite for CS 51500</li> <li> MA 518: Advanced Discrete Mathematics</li> <li> MA 520: Boundary Value Problems of Differential Equations </li> <li>MA 523: Introduction to Partial Differential Equations</li> <li>MA 525: Introduction to Complex Analysis</li> <li>MA 575: Linear Graph Theory</li> <li>MA 585: Mathematical Logic I</li> <li>MA 586: Mathematical Logic II</li> <li>MA 611: Methods of Applied Mathematics I</li> </ul> <ul><li>STAT 519: Introduction to Probability</li> <li>STAT 522: Sampling and Survey Techniques</li> <li>STAT 528: Introduction to Mathematical Statistics</li> <li>STAT 529: Applied Decision Theory and Bayesian Statistics</li> <li>STAT 598A: Introduction to Machine Learning</li> <li>STAT 598: Probabilistic Graph Models<br /> </li> <li></li> </ul> <p>Other courses, including courses at other institutions, may be approved on an individual basis. Please submit a course syllabus, along with comparison (if applicable) with courses in the department.</p> <p>The following courses are generally not approved by the graduate committee:</p> <p>Courses cross-listed as graduate and undergraduate courses, or offered for both graduate and undergraduate credit, unless primarily taken by graduate students</p> <p>The following Purdue courses:</p> <ul> <li>CPT 581F: Introduction to Computer Forensics (meets with 499F)</li> <li>ECE 608: Computational Models and Methods</li> <li>MA 511: Linear Algebra with Applications</li> <li>STAT 501: Experimental Statistics I</li> <li>STAT 502: Experimental Statistics II</li> <li>STAT 511: Statistical Methods</li> <li>STAT 512: Applied Regression Analysis</li> </ul> <!-- InstanceEndEditable --> <div style="height: 15px;
