<html> <head> <title>Network Systems Colloquium</title> </head> <BODY bgcolor="#DCDCDC"> <hr> <center> <h1> <font face="Helvetica"> Network Systems Colloquium </font> </h1> </center> <hr> <blockquote> <center> <font size=+1 face="Helvetica"> <strong> ANALYSIS OF MULTI-USER TCP CONNECTIONS <br> <br> Philippe Jacquet <br> INRIA </font> </center> </blockquote> </p> <blockquote> <center> <FONT COLOR="#000000" size=+1 face="Helvetica"> <strong> December 8, 1:30PM <br> CS 101 <br> (Note special date/time/room) </strong> </FONT> </center> </blockquote> <hr> <p> <center> <strong> Abstract </strong> </center> <p> We use the mean-field methodology in order to derive the asymptotic behavior of N TCP connection transiting through a buffer of finite size. We assume that the server are located on a very fast link and the clients are located on a relatively slower link. The two links are connected by a router with a buffer of finite size. The exchanged file are assumed infinite. We derive the asymptotic distribution of the buffer occupancy and of the window size when both the number of connections and the buffer size increase. <p> This is joint work with Cedric Adjih and Nikita Vvedenskaya. <p> <hr> <p> <em> The <a href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/nsl/colloquium.html"> Network Systems Colloquium</a> is sponsored by the <a href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/nsl">Network Systems Lab</a> at <a href="http://www.purdue.edu/"> Purdue University</a>.
