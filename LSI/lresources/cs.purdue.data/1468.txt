InstanceEndEditable --> </span> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="FeatureContent" --> <p><em>The following information is subject to change at any time without notice.</em></p> <p>To encourage graduate students to be well-informed about current research, department sponsors weekly lectures by faculty members and prominent visitors. All graduate students must register for CS 690S, Departmental Research Seminar, and attend at least 12 of these lectures each semester during their first year.</p> <p>Master's programs usually take three or four semesters. The practical maximum load is four courses per semester and two in the summer session. Students with assistantships rarely take more than three courses per semester and one in the summer session. Completing a master's program within twelve months is sometimes possible for well-prepared, industrious students.</p> <p><strong>Regular Master's Program</strong></p> <p>The regular master's program requires ten three-credit courses or eight with a thesis. CS 520, 565, and 580 are required of students who have not already had equivalent courses. The remaining courses are chosen by the student with the approval of the Graduate Committee. The use of independent courses (such as CS 590 and 690) is limited. In the non-thesis option, the limit is normally two courses (six credits). </p> <p><strong>Statistics-Computer Sciences Joint Master's Program</strong></p> <p>This special program is intended to prepare students for careers in industrial and research organizations. It consists of 33 credits of courses including all five courses from one of the following areas in statistics</p> <ul class="close_list"> <li>Experimental Design;STAT 514, 519, 525, 528, 529; </li> <li>Time Series;STAT 519, 520, 525, 528, 529; </li> <li>Sample Survey;STAT 519, 522, 525, 528, 529; </li> <li>Multivariate Statistics;STAT 519, 524, 525, 528, 529; </li> <li>Stochastic Processes;STAT 519, 525, 528, 529, 532; </li> </ul> <p>and all five courses from one of the following areas in computer sciences</p> <ul class="close_list"> <li>Performance Modeling;CS 510, 514, 543, 544, 536; </li> <li>Computer Systems;CS 502, 503, 536, 543, 544; </li> <li>Database Systems;CS 541, 542, 536, 543, 544; </li> <li>Statistical Computing;CS 514, 520, 535, 565, 614; </li> <li>Theory;CS 543, 580, 582, 565, 650.; </li> </ul> <p><strong>Computational Science and Engineering Specializations</strong></p> <p>The Department of Computer Sciences participates in the multidisciplinary <a href="http://www.cse.purdue.edu/"> Computational Science and Engineering Program.</a></p> <p><em>For the M.S. with a specialization in computational science and engineering,</em> the requirements are the same as for the regular master's program except that: </p> <ol class="close_list"> <li>The plan of study must include three CS&amp;E core courses other than CS 501.</li> <li>The plan of study must include three courses, or one course plus a thesis, in a single application area outside Computer Sciences. Appropriate CS&amp;E core courses may be used to fulfill application area requirements.</li> <li>At least 21 of the course credits on the plan of study must be in Computer Sciences. CS 501 may not be used.</li> <li>The GPA requirement for the plan of study is 3.25.</li> <li>The plan of study must be approved by the CS&amp;E Graduate Committee (as well as by the Computer Sciences Graduate Committee and the student's advisory committee).</li> </ol> <p>CS 520, 565, and 580 remain as required Computer Sciences core courses. CS 520 may also be used toward the CS&amp;E core requirement.</p> <p>Credit for graduate courses taken at other institutions may be transferred with the approval of the Graduate Committee and the Graduate School. Courses that have been used to fulfill the requirements for other degrees will not be approved, except in certain circumstances courses used for a Ph.D. Application for transfer is made when the plan of study is presented for approval. On master's plans of study, transferred credits are normally limited to six semester-hours.</p> <p>Plans of study may, with the approval of the Graduate Committee, include courses related to computer science, typically courses in electrical engineering, computer engineering, industrial engineering, mathematics, and statistics. On master's plans, outside courses are normally limited to two.</p> <!-- InstanceEndEditable --> <div style="height: 15px; width: 10px;"></div> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceEndRepeat --> <p>Contact Us: <a href="mailto:grad-info@cs.purdue.edu">grad-info@cs.purdue.edu</a><br /> <span style="color: #777">Last Updated: June 12, 2009 12:09PM</span></p> <div style="height: 15px; width: 10px;"></div> </div> <div id="info"> <!-- InstanceBeginRepeat name="InfoBoxes" --><!-- InstanceBeginRepeatEntry --> <div> <span class="header"> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Info Title" -->Masters Requirements<!-- InstanceEndEditable --> </span> <!-- InstanceBeginRepeat name="InfoLinks" --><!-- InstanceBeginRepeatEntry --> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Link Text" --><a href="../masters.sxhtml">Current Master's</a><!-- InstanceEndEditable --> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceBeginRepeatEntry --> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Link Text" --> <a href="ms_2001.sxhtml">2001 Master's </a><!-- InstanceEndEditable --> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceBeginRepeatEntry --> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Link Text" --> <a href="ms_1999.sxhtml">2000 Master's </a><!-- InstanceEndEditable --> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceBeginRepeatEntry --> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Link Text" --> <a href="ms_1999.sxhtml">Pre Fall 1999 Master's </a><!-- InstanceEndEditable --> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceEndRepeat --> </div> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceEndRepeat --> </div> </div> <div style="clear: both;"></div> <div id="footer"> <div style="font-size: 80%;font-weight: 800;float: left;"> Computer Sciences, 305 N.
