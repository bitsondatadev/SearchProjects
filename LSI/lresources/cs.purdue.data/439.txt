href="/report/">Corrections &amp; Updates</a> </li> </ul> </div> <div id="feature"> <span class="feature_title">Printer Usage Policy</span> <p> The Department of Computer Sciences has several "public" laser printers available for use by those with accounts on department machines. For a list of "public" printers available from a given UNIX machine, use <b>lpinfo</b>(L). If you plan to (continue to) use the department's printers, you should become aware of the policy governing their use.</p> <p> The printers are a shared resource. The PostScript printers are mechanical devices typically designed for lighter work loads than they receive here. It is essential to their health that they only be used as necessary. The printers are to be used for the same purposes as your account -- research work and/or administrative or course work for which your account was assigned. You should not be using the printers for personal use. It is considered appropriate for students to print resumes, but only single copies and only your own.</p> <p> You should only print manual pages on the printers as absolutely necessary<a href="#FN1">[1]</a>.</p> <p> You should not print documents just to "see what they are." There are on-line previewers available essentially all documentation formats. These are excellent tools for proofing documents, and generally save time, paper, and printers.</p> <p> You should not pipe program output to <b>lpr</b> unless you have a good idea of how much output you are going to generate and the amount is less than about 20 pages.</p> <p> You should not use the printers as duplicating machines. If you want multiple copies of a document, print one copy and then use a photocopy machine to make more copies.</p> <p> Filing output is everyone's responsibility. Where output trays are available, all output should be filed by the FIRST letter of the login on the banner page. Where output bins are not available, stack output neatly on the table. Be careful to separate the output of different users. Output filed in the wrong bin is a frequent cause of "lost output".</p> <p> If you are printing output on the printer that is more than 25 pages, you should be there to collect it when it comes out. The output trays on the printers are not designed to handle large numbers of pages, and it is necessary to remove some of the output before it is finished. When someone else besides the owner does this, the portions frequently end up scattered around the tables without header pages.</p> <p> When you direct output to the printer, make sure you pick it up. Even if you no longer want it, you should collect the output to avoid unecessary clutter in the output bins or on the output table. You should also recycle paper that you do not want. Printer paper should go in the small grey recycling bins.</p> <p /> <hr /> <ol> <li><a name="FN1"></a> For local use only, some of the manuals are subject to copyright restrictions.</li> </ol> <div style="height: 15px; width: 10px;"></div> <p> Contact Us: <a href="mailto:software@cs.purdue.edu">Facilities Software Staff</a><br /> <span style="color: #777">Last Updated: September 23, 2010 11:00AM</span > </p> <div style="height: 15px; width: 10px;"></div> </div> <div id="info"> <div> <span class="header">Purdue University Policies</span> <a href="http://www.purdue.edu/policies/">Purdue Policies Homepage</a> <a href="http://www.purdue.edu/policies/records/viiia4.html">Purdue FERPA Policy</a> </div> <div> <span class="header">Purdue IT Policies</span> <a href="http://www.purdue.edu/policies/information-technology.html">IT Policies Home</a> <a href="http://www.purdue.edu/policies/information-technology/viib1.html">Authentication and Authorization (VII.B.1)</a> <a href="http://www.purdue.edu/policies/information-technology/viib2.html">Privacy for Electronic Information (VII.B.2)</a> <a href="http://www.purdue.edu/policies/information-technology/c-34.html">Data Security and Access Policy (C-34)</a> <a href="http://www.purdue.edu/policies/information-technology/c-42.html">Purdue Pages on the World Wide Web, Policy for (C-42)</a> <a href="http://www.purdue.edu/policies/information-technology/viia1.html">Electronic Mail (VII.A.1)</a> </div> </div> </div> <div style="clear: both;" /> <div id="footer"> <div style="font-size: 80%;font-weight: 800;float: left;"> Computer Sciences, 305 N.
