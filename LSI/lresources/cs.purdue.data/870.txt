He obtained his Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from Brown University, and M.S. degree as well as Ph.D. degree at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. His research is primarily in the area of 3D computer graphics but overlaps with visualization and with computer vision. His research area is of central importance to many important industries, including computer-aided design and manufacturing, telepresence, scientific simulations, and education. Aliaga has also served on several NSF panels, chaired and served on numerous ACM and IEEE conference and workshop committees, is on the editorial board of Graphical Models, and is a member of ACM SIGGRAPH.
