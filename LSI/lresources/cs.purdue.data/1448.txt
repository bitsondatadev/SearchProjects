InstanceEndEditable --> </span> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="FeatureContent" --> <p>These requirements apply to all students entering or reentering the Department of Computer Sciences at West Lafayette (&quot;the Department&quot;) as degree-seeking graduate students in the fall semester of 2000 or later.</p> <p>Students entering or reentering in the fall semester of 2000 may apply to the Graduate Committee to follow in their entirety the <a href="ms_1999.sxhtml">former master's degree requirements</a> and all other degree requirements in effect at the end of the spring semester of 2000.</p> <p>Students who entered or reentered prior to the fall semester of 2000 may apply to the Graduate Committee to follow in their entirety the 2000 requirements and all other degree requirements in effect at the beginning of the fall semester of 2000.</p> <hr /> <p><em>The following information is subject to change at any time without notice.</em></p> <p>To encourage graduate students to be well-informed about current research, the department sponsors weekly lectures by faculty members and prominent visitors. All graduate students must register for CS 690S, Departmental Research Seminar, and attend at least 12 of these lectures each semester during their first year.</p> <p>Master's programs usually take three or four semesters. The practical maximum load is four courses per semester and two in the summer session. Students with assistantships rarely take more than three courses per semester and one in the summer session. Completing a master's program within twelve months is sometimes possible for well-prepared, industrious students.</p> <p>Courses, other than CS 690S, used to fulfill degree requirements must be listed on a plan of study and submitted for approval by the Graduate Committee and the Graduate School well before the final session. Grades of A or B are expected, but one or two C's may be accepted if they are compensated by A's. Other grades are unacceptable.</p> <p>Up to six semester-hours of credit for graduate courses taken at other institutions may be transferred with the approval of the Graduate Committee and the Graduate School. Application for transfer is made when the plan of study is submitted for approval.</p> <p>Courses used to fulfill the requirements for other degrees (at Purdue or elsewhere) are not eligible for use on master's plans of study, except that courses used for a doctoral degree may be used on a master's plan of study provided the doctoral plan of study does not include any course used for any other master's degree.</p> <p><strong>Regular Master's Program</strong></p> <p>The regular master's program requires ten three-credit courses or eight with a thesis. These include: </p> <ul> <li>Three core courses: CS 502 or 565, CS 503 or 536, and CS 580. These represent the areas Systems I, Systems II, and Algorithms in the table below.</li> <li>Four other courses from the table, including at least two from areas other than Systems I, Systems II, and Algorithms.</li> <li>Under the <em>non-thesis option</em>, three more 500- or 600-level courses (not necessarily in Computer Sciences), at most two of which may be independent study courses. Under the <em>thesis option</em>, one more 500- or 600-level course (not necessarily in Computer Sciences), which may <em>not</em> be an independent study course. Courses from outside the department must be approved by the Graduate Committee.</li> </ul> <a name="Areas" id="Areas"></a> <table class="basic_table"> <tr> <th>Area</th> <th>Course(s)</th> <th>&nbsp;</th> </tr> <tr> <td>Algorithms</td> <td>CS 580</td> <td><br /></td> </tr> <tr> <td>Artificial Intelligence</td> <td>CS 572</td> <td><br /></td> </tr> <tr> <td>Complexity</td> <td>CS 584</td> <td><br /></td> </tr> <tr> <td>Databases </td> <td>CS 541, 542</td> <td><br /></td> </tr> <tr> <td>Geometric Modeling, Visualization, and Graphics</td> <td>CS 530, 531, 586</td> <td><br /></td> </tr> <tr> <td>Numerical Computing</td> <td>CS 514, 515, 520,</td> <td>614, 615</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Parallel and Distributed Computing</td> <td>CS 525,</td> <td>603</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Security</td> <td>CS 526, 555</td> <td><br /></td> </tr> <tr> <td>Simulation and Modeling</td> <td>CS 543, 544</td> <td><br /></td> </tr> <tr> <td>Software Engineering</td> <td>CS 510</td> <td><br /></td> </tr> <tr> <td>Systems I (Compilers and Programming Languages)</td> <td>CS 502, 565</td> <td><br /></td> </tr> <tr> <td>Systems II (Networks and Operating Systems)</td> <td>CS 503, 536,</td> <td>636</td> </tr> </table> <p><strong>This table is the same as that used for the doctoral program except that Algorithms and Complexity are separate areas here.</strong></p> <p>Students may ask the Graduate Committee to accept equivalent graduate courses taken at other institutions in lieu of at most two of the above courses.</p> <p><strong>Statistics-Computer Sciences Joint Master's Program</strong></p> <p>This special program is intended to prepare students for careers in industrial and research organizations. It consists of 33 credits of courses including all five courses from one of the following areas in statistics </p> <ul class="close_list"> <li>Experimental Design: STAT 514, 519, 525, 528, 529; </li> <li>Time Series: STAT 519, 520, 525, 528, 529; </li> <li>Sample Survey: STAT 519, 522, 525, 528, 529; </li> <li>Multivariate Statistics: STAT 519, 524, 525, 528, 529; </li> <li>Stochastic Processes: STAT 519, 525, 528, 529, 532; </li> </ul> <p>and all five courses from one of the following areas in computer sciences</p> <ul> <li>Performance Modeling: CS 510, 514, 536, 543, 544; </li> <li>Computer Systems: CS 502, 503, 536, 543, 544; </li> <li>Database Systems: CS 536, 541, 542, 543, 544; </li> <li>Statistical Computing: CS 514, 520, 535, 565, 614.</li> </ul> <p><strong>Computational Science and Engineering Specialization</strong></p> <p>The Department of Computer Sciences participates in the multidisciplinary <a href="http://www.cse.purdue.edu/">Computational Science and Engineering Program.</a></p> <p>For the M.S. with a specialization in computational science and engineering (CS&amp;E), the requirements are the same as for the regular master's program except that:</p> <ol> <li>The plan of study must include three CS&amp;E core courses other than CS 501. </li> <li>The plan of study must include three courses, or one course plus a thesis, in a single application area outside Computer Sciences. Appropriate CS&amp;E core courses may be used to fulfill application area requirements. </li> <li>At least 21 of the course credits on the plan of study must be in Computer Sciences. CS 501 may not be used. </li> <li>The GPA requirement for the plan of study is 3.25.</li> <li>The plan of study must be approved by the CS&amp;E Graduate Committee (as well as by the Computer Sciences Graduate Committee and the student's advisory committee).</li> </ol> <!-- InstanceEndEditable --> <div style="height: 15px; width: 10px;"></div> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceEndRepeat --> <p>Contact Us: <a href="mailto:grad-info@cs.purdue.edu">grad-info@cs.purdue.edu</a><br /> <span style="color: #777">Last Updated: June 12, 2009 12:09PM</span></p> <div style="height: 15px; width: 10px;"></div> </div> <div id="info"> <!-- InstanceBeginRepeat name="InfoBoxes" --><!-- InstanceBeginRepeatEntry --> <div> <span class="header"> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Info Title" -->Masters Requirements<!-- InstanceEndEditable --> </span> <!-- InstanceBeginRepeat name="InfoLinks" --><!-- InstanceBeginRepeatEntry --> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Link Text" --><a href="../masters.sxhtml">Current Master's</a><!-- InstanceEndEditable --> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceBeginRepeatEntry --> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Link Text" --> <a href="ms_2001.sxhtml">2001 Master's </a><!-- InstanceEndEditable --> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceBeginRepeatEntry --> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Link Text" --> <a href="ms_2000.sxhtml">2000 Master's</a><!-- InstanceEndEditable --> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceBeginRepeatEntry --> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Link Text" --> <a href="ms_1999.sxhtml">Pre Fall 1999 Master's </a><!-- InstanceEndEditable --> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceEndRepeat --> </div> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceEndRepeat --> </div> </div> <div style="clear: both;"></div> <div id="footer"> <div style="font-size: 80%;font-weight: 800;float: left;"> Computer Sciences, 305 N.
