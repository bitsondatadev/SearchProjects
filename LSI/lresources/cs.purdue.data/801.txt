The designers of CSNET, the Computer Science Network, were awarded the <a href="http://isoc.org/wp/newsletter/?p=1098">2009 Internet Society (ISOC) Postel Award</a>.&nbsp; The award was announced July 29, 2009 at the ISOC meeting in Stockholm, Sweden.</p> <p class="style1">The Postel Award recognizes the four principle investigators and their teams for the development of CSNET. The PI&rsquo;s of the NSF-funded project in 1981 were Prof. Peter Denning (Purdue), Prof. David Farber (Delaware), Dr. Anthony Hearn (RAND), and Prof. Lawrence Landweber (Wisconsin). </p> <p class="style1"> Research at Purdue was led by Denning and Prof. Douglas Comer. Comer was responsible for the design and development of the software that allowed the use of the new TCP/IP protocols over the X.25 commercial packet-switched network.</p> </td> </tr> </table> </body> </html> </html>
