</span> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="FeatureContent" --> <p>These requirements apply to all students entering or reentering the department of Computer Sciences at West Lafayette (&quot;the department&quot;) as degree-seeking graduate students in the fall semester of 1999 or later.</p> <p>Students entering or reentering in the fall semester of 1999 may apply to the Graduate Committee to follow in their entirety the 1992 qualifying requirements and all other degree requirements in effect at the end of the spring semester of 1999.</p> <p>Students who entered or reentered prior to the fall semester of 1999 may apply to the Graduate Committee to follow in their entirety the 1999 qualifying requirements and all other degree requirements in effect at the beginning of the fall semester of 1999.</p> <p>For a list of the differences between these and the earlier requirements <a href="phd_1992.sxhtml"> click here.</a></p> <hr /> <p><em>The following information is subject to change at any time without notice.</em></p> <p>The doctoral program requires at least 12 three-credit courses, successful completion of a qualifying process, passage of a preliminary examination, successful defense of a dissertation, and deposit of the dissertation in the Library. Two semesters of the departmental research seminar are also required.</p> <p><strong>Qualifying Process.</strong> For a description of the qualifying process, <a href="phd_1999_qual.sxhtml"> click here.</a></p> <p><strong>Advisory Committee and Plan of Study.</strong> By the end of the semester in which the student completes the qualifying process, the student must form an advisory committee and file a plan of study approved by this committee and the Graduate Committee. To form the committee, the student first finds a faculty member who agrees to serve as chair of the committee; the chair and student then select two additional faculty members who are willing to serve on the committee.</p> <p><strong>The plan of study</strong> must include seven courses (21 credits), including all those taken in the department to qualify, from at least five areas in the table given in the requirements for qualifying. No grade lower than B is acceptable, and the GPA in the seven courses must be at least 3.5. The seven courses must be completed by the end of the fourth semester in the doctoral program.</p> <p>The plan of study must include at least five more 500- or 600-level courses (15 credits), other than independent study and seminar courses, approved by the student's the advisory committee and the Graduate Committee and relevant to the student's research. These courses need not be taken in the department or even at Purdue, but they cannot have been used to satisfy requirements for an undergraduate degree nor can they cause a doctoral plan of study to include courses from more than one master's program. The grades in these five courses must be A or B, except for at most six credits of C.</p> <p><strong>Preliminary Examination.</strong> The preliminary examination tests the student's competence in a research area and readiness for research on some specific problem. The examining committee normally consists of the student's advisory committee and an additional member chosen by the Graduate Committee. The content of the examination is at the discretion of the examining committee. The examination may consist, for example, of a presentation by the student of papers relevant to a research topic agreed upon by the student and the committee; or it may consist of a proposal for thesis research; or it may involve an oral examination over the material in appropriate courses beyond the qualifier level This examination is to be taken by the end of the third semester following the one in which the student passes the qualifying examination and at least two semesters before the examination on the dissertation.</p> <p><strong>Dissertation.</strong> The dissertation must present new results worthy of publication. The student must defend the dissertation publicly and to the satisfaction of the examining committee, which normally consists of the student's advisory committee and one additional faculty member representing an area outside that of the dissertation. The dissertation should be completed by the end of the fourth semester following the one in which the student passes the preliminary examination. The Graduate Committee may grant extensions.</p> <p><strong>Computational Science and Engineering Specializations</strong></p> <p> The Department of Computer Sciences participates in the multidisciplinary <a href="http://www.cse.purdue.edu/"> Computational Science and Engineering Program.</a></p> <p><em>For the Ph.D. with a specialization in computational science and engineering,</em> the requirements are the same as for the regular doctoral program except that:</p> <ol> <li>The student must take five of CS 502, 503, 514, 520, 565, 580, and 584, including: at least two of CS 502, 503, and 565; at least one of CS 514 and 520; and at least one of CS 580 and CS 584. CS 514 and 520 may also be used toward the CS&amp;E core requirement.</li> <li>The plan of study must include four CS&amp;E core courses other than CS 501.</li> <li>The plan of study must include four courses in a single application area outside Computer Sciences. Appropriate CS&amp;E core courses may be used to fulfill application area requirements.</li> <li>At least one half of the course credits on the plan of study must be in Computer Sciences. CS 501 may not be used.</li> <li>The GPA requirement for the plan of study is 3.25.</li> <li>No course with a grade of C can be included on the plan of study.</li> <li>The plan of study must be approved by the CS&amp;E Graduate Committee (as well as by the Computer Sciences Graduate Committee and the student's advisory committee).</li> <li>Part 2 of the qualifying examination will contain a component, determined by the Computer Sciences and CS&amp;E graduate committees, covering the application area. </li> </ol> <p> The dissertation is done under CS 699. </p> <!-- InstanceEndEditable --> <div style="height: 15px;
