Specialization in Software Engineering<!-- InstanceEndEditable --> </span> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="FeatureContent" --> <p>&nbsp;</p> <p><strong>SE Major</strong></p> <p>Students apply to enter this interdisciplinary major at the end of their sophomore year.&nbsp; Students are selected by the Software Engineering Curriculum Committee based on a solid grounding in programming (at least a B in CS 18000 and in CS 24000) and the results of an interview with the Committee. Formal acceptance is at the beginning of the junior year, following the student's first internship.</p> <p>The requirements are the same as for the CS Major but with the four CS electives replaced by the following:</p> <p>1. CS 30700, Software Engineering (3 cr.)</p> <p>2. Twelve or more credits from the courses listed below, including at least six credits of CS courses and at least six credits of non-CS courses.&nbsp; Some of the courses are still in development and may be&nbsp;&nbsp; given as temporary courses (e.g. CS 49000).<br /> &nbsp; <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; * Software Design:<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ECE 43500 (Object-Oriented Design Using C++ and Java, 3 cr.)<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; * Embedded Systems: <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ECE 36200 (Microprocessor Systems and Interfacing, 4 cr.)<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; * Software Testing and Quality Assurance<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;CS course<br /> &nbsp; <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; * Information Security: <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;CS 35500 (Introduction to Cryptography, 3 cr.)<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;CS 42600 (Computer Security, 3 cr.)<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ECE course in security<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; * Information Systems: <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;CS 34800 (Information Systems, 3 cr.)</p> <p>&nbsp;&nbsp; * Software Architecture:<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;CS course<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; * Software Process Management: <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;IE course<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; * Projects<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Courses from EPICS or the VIP program.&nbsp; Must be approved by the Software <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Engineering Curriculum Committee.&nbsp; Limited to 3 credits.&nbsp; The projects must be <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;software or software/hardware intensive.</p> <p>3. Internships</p> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Two summer internships of at least eight weeks, each in a commercial software <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;development group provided by a Corporate Partner.&nbsp; The responsibility of <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;finding the internships lies with the student.&nbsp; Students that cannot secure the <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;two internships but fulfill all other requirements for the SE Major will be <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;awarded the BS degree with the CS Major. <p>&nbsp;4. SE Seminar</p> <p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; This one-credit seminar must be taken in the senior or preferably junior year.</p> <p><strong>Sample Plan of Study </strong><br /> <em>[Note: The interdisciplinary specialization in SE will be a track for CS.
