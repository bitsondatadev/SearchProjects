<div class="level1"> </div> <!-- SECTION "Travel Grants Program" [1-40] --> <h2><a name="overview" id="overview">Overview</a></h2> <div class="level2"> <p> Travel grants are provided through a joint effort of the Computer Science Graduate Student Board and the <a href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/" class="urlextern" title="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/" rel="nofollow">Department of Computer Science</a>. The spirit of the <acronym title="Graduate Student Board">GSB</acronym> Travel Grant is intended to provide partial financial support for graduate students presenting their research at <em>high-quality</em> conferences when their <em>academic advisor is unable</em> to provide funding. Grants usually amount to $500, which can only be used for transportation, hotel, and conference admission. There is a limit of one grant per paper. The program is currently sponsored through the <a href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/corp/" class="urlextern" title="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/corp/" rel="nofollow">Corporate partners</a> program of the Department of Computer Science. This fund is quite limited and is to be seen as a <strong>last resort</strong>, not as an alternative to other available travel money. It is expected that students pursue all available funding opportunities and are able to document the sources tried. These sources, for example, include: </p> <ol> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> Purdue Graduate Student Government (<a href="http://web.ics.purdue.edu/~pgsg/" class="urlextern" title="http://web.ics.purdue.edu/~pgsg/" rel="nofollow">PGSG</a>) offers travel grants each semester.</div> </li> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> A number of conferences offer student travel grants and give preference to authors of accepted papers.</div> </li> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> For security-related research, Center for Education in Information Assurance and Security (<a href="http://www.cerias.purdue.edu/" class="urlextern" title="http://www.cerias.purdue.edu/" rel="nofollow">CERIAS</a>) offers student travel support.</div> </li> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> The Women in Science Program (<a href="http://www.science.purdue.edu/WISP/Resources/travel.html" class="urlextern" title="http://www.science.purdue.edu/WISP/Resources/travel.html" rel="nofollow">WISP</a>) and <a href="http://www.science.purdue.edu/prospective_students/graduate/agep.asp" class="urlextern" title="http://www.science.purdue.edu/prospective_students/graduate/agep.asp" rel="nofollow">AGEP</a> initiative offer student travel support.</div> </li> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> Fellowships might have funds for student travel.</div> </li> </ol> </div> <!-- SECTION "Overview" [41-1700] --> <h2><a name="eligibility" id="eligibility">Eligibility</a></h2> <div class="level2"> <ol> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> The work must have been performed while a member of the Department of Computer Science.</div> </li> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> The student must be the presenter of the paper.</div> </li> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> The student must be a degree seeking graduate student in the Department of Computer Science.</div> </li> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> The student&#039;s academic advisor must not have funds to support the travel (confirmed by the <a href="http://www2.cs.purdue.edu/busoff/" class="urlextern" title="http://www2.cs.purdue.edu/busoff/" rel="nofollow">CS Business Office</a> and the advisor).</div> </li> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> It must be the case that no other student from the Department of Computer Science is funded to go to the conference because of the same paper.</div> </li> </ol> </div> <!-- SECTION "Eligibility" [1701-2285] --> <h2><a name="evaluation_process" id="evaluation_process">Evaluation Process</a></h2> <div class="level2"> <p> The number of the grants issued depends on the availability of the funds. The evaluation process is based on the following (and possibly other) criteria: </p> <ul> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> Quality of the conference </div> </li> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> Quality of the paper </div> </li> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> Conference acceptance rate </div> </li> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> Student&#039;s record </div> </li> </ul> <p> Note: There is no consideration of domestic versus international conferences. </p> <p> All applications will be evaluated by a committee consisting of two students and two faculty members. The faculty advisor of the <acronym title="Graduate Student Board">GSB</acronym> is the chair of the evaluation committee. The committee&#039;s decision will be passed to the Department Head for final approval. </p> </div> <!-- SECTION "Evaluation Process" [2286-2931] --> <h2><a name="application_process" id="application_process">Application Process</a></h2> <div class="level2"> <p> Each applicant needs to submit the following items: </p> <ol> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> Completed <a href="/gsb/_media/docs:travel_grant_app.pdf?id=travel_grants&amp;cache=cache" class="media mediafile mf_pdf" title="docs:travel_grant_app.pdf">Application Form</a> / <a href="/gsb/_media/docs:application.doc?id=travel_grants&amp;cache=cache" class="media mediafile mf_doc" title="docs:application.doc">Application Form (WORD)</a></div> </li> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> A nomination letter from the advisor stating:</div> <ol> <li class="level2"><div class="li"> Why he/she cannot fund the trip with his/her research grants or unrestricted gift funds</div> </li> <li class="level2"><div class="li"> Who will pay the remaining amount of the total travel cost</div> </li> </ol> </li> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> Call for participation for the conference that includes the members of the program committee</div> </li> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> Acceptance letter from the program chair with paper reviews</div> </li> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> The paper itself</div> </li> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> Applicant&#039;s vita or resume</div> </li> </ol> <p> Submission: Please e-mail all materials (in <acronym title="Portable Document Format">PDF</acronym> format) to the <acronym title="Graduate Student Board">GSB</acronym> Travel Grant Coordinator (see <a href="/gsb/officers" class="wikilink1" title="officers">officers</a> page). If you cannot e-mail all the materials, please e-mail the <acronym title="Graduate Student Board">GSB</acronym> Travel Grant Coordinator for other arrangements. </p> </div> <!-- SECTION "Application Process" [2932-3781] --> <h2><a name="deadlines" id="deadlines">Deadlines</a></h2> <div class="level2"> <p> As of Summer 2007, this program has an open-deadline. However, applications should be received within two weeks of the paper&#039;s acceptance and before the actual travel. </p> </div> <!-- SECTION "Deadlines" [3782-3975] --> <h2><a name="notifications" id="notifications">Notifications</a></h2> <div class="level2"> <p> You should be contacted within 3 days of submission by the <acronym title="Graduate Student Board">GSB</acronym> Travel Grant Coordinator confirming the receipt of the application. If not, please contact (him/her) via e-mail. </p> <p> The applications should be evaluated during a period of about two weeks to four weeks, and all applicants will be notified at the end of that period. However, depending on the time of year or scheduling conflicts, the evaluation of an application may take longer. </p> </div> <!-- SECTION "Notifications" [3976-4447] --> <h2><a name="past_recipients" id="past_recipients">Past Recipients</a></h2> <div class="level2"> </div> <!-- SECTION "Past Recipients" [4448-4481] --> <h3><a name="fall_2008" id="fall_2008">Fall 2008</a></h3> <div class="level3"> <ul> <li class="level1"><div class="li"> J.
