Spafford</H1> <IMG SRC="goldRule.gif" width =100%> <P> <EM>Professor of Computer Sciences (1987)</EM> <P> <EM> B.A., mathematics and computer science, <a href="http://www.brockport.edu">SUNY College at Brockport</a>, 1979; M.S., information and computer science, <a href="http://www.gatech.edu">Georgia Institute of Technology</a>, 1981; Ph.D., information and computer science, <a href="http://www.gatech.edu">Georgia Institute of Technology</a>, 1986</EM> <P> Before coming to Purdue, Dr. Spafford was a research scientist with the Software Engineering Research Center at <a href="http://www.gatech.edu">Georgia Institute of Technology</a>. While there, he participated in research efforts in software testing, software engineering liability, and distributed computing (the Clouds project). <P> Dr. Spafford's current research interests are focused on issues of computer and network security, computer crime and ethics, and the social impact of computing. He is the founder and director of the <a href="/coast">COAST Project</a> -- the world's largest academic research group dedicated to issues of practical information security. His other interests include software engineering, electronic message systems, and computer science education. <P> Among many professional activities, Dr. Spafford is a member of the ACM's <a href="http://www.acm.org/usacm">U.S. Public Policy Committee</a> and he is a member of the advisory board of the <a href="http://www.scsu-cs.ctstateu.edu/rccs/">National Research Center on Computing and Society</a>. In 1996, he was named a charter recipient of the Computer Society's <em><A HREF="http://www.computer.org/awards/Goldcore.htm">Golden Core</A></em>, for his past service to the Society. Dr. Spafford is on the editorial and advisory boards of the <cite><a href="http://www.virusbtn.com/">Virus Bulletin</a></cite>, the journal <cite><a href="http://www.elsevier.nl/locate/405877">Computers &amp; Security</a></cite>, <cite><a href="http://www-mitpress.mit.edu/jrnls-catalog/artificial.html"> Journal of Artificial Life</a></cite>, <cite><a href="http://www.elsevier.nl:80/locate/inca/30358">Network Security</a></cite>, and the <cite>Journal of Information Systems Security</cite>. <P> Dr. Spafford is coauthor of the recent award-winning book <a href="http://www.ora.com/item/pus2.html"><cite>Practical Unix &amp; Internet Security</cite></a> (with S. L. Garfinkel), published by <a href="http://www.ora.com">O'Reilly and Associates</a> (1991, 1996). He was a contributing editor and author on <cite><a href="http://www.ora.com/item/crime.html">Computer Crime: A Crime-Fighters Handbook</a></cite> (1995), and <a href="http://www.ora.com/catalog/websec"><cite>Web Security and Commerce</cite></a> (1997), both published by <a href="http://www.ora.com">O'Reilly</a>. <P> <i>Labs:</i> <UL> <li> <A
