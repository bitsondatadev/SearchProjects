General Index Framework for Space Partitioning Trees</FONT></B></FONT></U></STRONG> </P> <P></P> <P align="justify">Emerging database applications require the use of new indexing structures beyond B-trees and R-trees. Examples are the k-d tree, the trie, the quadtree, and their variants. They are often proposed as supporting structures in data mining, GIS, CAD/CAM and biological database applications. A common feature of all these indexes is that they recursively divide the space into partitions. </P> <P align="justify">SP-GiST Version 2 is an indexing tool that works under PostgreSQL. It allows the creation of non-traditional disk-based indexes that belong to the class of space-partitioning trees to serve the needs of emerging database applications.. Examples of indexes one can instantiate in PostgreSQL using SP-GiST are disk-based versions of the trie and its variants (e.g., patricia tries), quad-trees and their variants (e.g., the point quad-tree, the PMR quad-tree, and the oct-tree), and k-d trees.&nbsp;</P> <P align="justify">&nbsp;</P> </td> <td width="3%" bgColor="white">&nbsp;</td> </tr> </table> </td> </tr> <tr> <td bgColor="white"> <div align="center"> </div> </td> </tr> </table> </div> </XSL:FOR-EACH></XSL:TEMPLATE></XSL:STYLESHEET> </body> </html> </html>
