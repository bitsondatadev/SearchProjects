Software seems to have has permeated almost every equipment that we use in our daily lives. Companies that produce embedded systems for use in health care, transportation, and other critical segments of our society have embraced model based software testing by integrating them into their development environments.</b></font></p> <p><font size="2" color="black" face="Courier New, Courier, Monaco, monospace"><b>This course is designed to enable a clear understanding and knowledge of the foundations, techniques, and tools in the area of software testing and its practice in the industry. The course will prepare students to be leaders in software testing. Whether you are a developer or a tester, you must test software. This course is a unique opportunity to learn strengths and weaknesses of a variety of software testing techniques.</b></font></p> <p><font size="2" color="black" face="Courier New, Courier, Monaco, monospace"><b>Applications of testing techniques in health care industry (e.g. pacemaker), nuclear industry (e.g. plant control), aerospace industry (e.g. Mars Polar Lander), security (e.g. smart card), automobile industry (e.g. automotive control systems), and others will be considered.</b></font></p> <p><font size="2" color="black" face="Courier New, Courier, Monaco, monospace"><b>The course will focus on: </b></font></p> <p><font size="2" color="black" face="Courier New, Courier, Monaco, monospace"><b>(a) Test process and continuous quality improvement</b></font></p> <p><font size="2" color="black" face="Courier New, Courier, Monaco, monospace"><b>(b) Test generation from requirements</b></font></p> <p><font size="2" color="black" face="Courier New, Courier, Monaco, monospace"><b>(c) Modeling techniques: UML: FSM and Statecharts, Combinatorial design; and others. </b></font></p> <p><font size="2" color="black" face="Courier New, Courier, Monaco, monospace"><b>(d) Test generation from models.</b></font></p> <p><font size="2" color="black" face="Courier New, Courier, Monaco, monospace"><b>(e) Test adequacy assessment.</b></font></p> <p><font size="2" color="black" face="Courier New, Courier, Monaco, monospace"><b>(f) Industrial applications.</b></font></p> <p><font size="2" color="black" face="Courier New, Courier, Monaco, monospace"><b>Discussion oriented lectures by the instructor, in-class group presentations by teams, laboratory exercises using advanced testing tools, and invited talks by experts from the industry will be the primary mechanisms for learning and the dissemination of knowledge.</b></font></p> <p><font size="2" color="#3300ff" face="Courier New, Courier, Monaco, monospace"><b>Course description:</b></font></p> <p><font size="2" color="black"><b>Fundamentals of software testing; software test proces and continuous quality improvement; Test generation using finite state models, Combinatorial design, and others; Test adequacy assessment using black box and white box criteria; Industrial applications of model based testing. Students will be required to form small teams of three or four, preferably interdisciplinary, and make presentations to the class.The work of each team will be reviewed by the instructor and other teams.</b></font></p> <p><font size="2" color="#000099" face="Courier New, Courier, Monaco, monospace">Prerequisites: Senior </font><font size="2" face="Courier New, Courier, Monaco, monospace">in CS or ECE. </font></p> <p><font size="2" color="#000099" face="Courier New, Courier, Monaco, monospace">Credits: 3</font></p> <p>Textbook: <a title="Foundations of Software Testing, Draft V3.0" href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/homes/apm/foundationsBook/index.html">Foundations of Software Testing,</a> Draft V3.0.
