Hoffmann</span> <div style="margin: 0"> <div style="margin: 0 0 10px 15px;"> Professor of Computer Science<br /> Codirector, PLM Center of Excellence <p> <em>Joined department: 1976</em> </p> </div> <div> <strong>Education:</strong> <div style="margin: 5px 5px 0px 15px;"> PhD, University of Wisconsin (1974) </div> </div><br style="clear: both;" /> <div> <p> Before joining the Purdue faculty, Professor Hoffmann taught at the University of Waterloo, Canada. He has also been a visiting professor at the Christian-Albrechts University in Kiel, West Germany (1980), and at Cornell University (1984-1986). His research focuses on geometric and solid modeling, its applications to manufacturing and science, and the simulation of physical systems. The research includes, in particular, research on geometric constraint solving and the semantics of generative, feature-based design. Professor Hoffmann is the author of <em>Group-Theoretic Algorithms and Graph Isomorphism</em>, <em>Lecture Notes in Computer Science</em>, 136, Springer-Verlag and of <em>Geometric and Solid Modeling: An Introduction</em>, published by Morgan Kaufmann, Inc. Professor Hoffmann has received national media attention for his work simulating the <a target="new_window" href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/homes/cmh/simulation/">9/11 attacks</a> on the Pentagon and the World Trade Center. </p> </div> <div> <strong>Selected Publications</strong> <div style="margin: 0 0 10px 0;"> S.-C.
