<HTML> <HEAD> <TITLE>Mikhail Atallah</TITLE> </HEAD> <BODY BGCOLOR="WHITE"> <P> Professor Atallah's main research interest is algorithms, in particular for computer security, geometry, and parallel computation. He received a Presidential Young Investigator Award from the National Science Foundation in 1985. A Fellow of the IEEE, he has served on the editorial boards of <i>SIAM Journal on Computing</i>, <i>Journal of Parallel and Distributed Computing</i>, <i>Information Processing Letters, Computational Geometry: Theory & Applications</i>, <i>International Journal of Computational Geometry & Applications</i>, <i>Parallel Processing Letters, Methods of Logic in Computer Science</i>. He was Guest Editor for a Special Issue of <i>Algorithmica</i> on Computational Geometry, has served as Editor of the <i>Handbook of Parallel and Distributed Computing</i> (McGraw-Hill), as Editorial Advisor for the <i>Handbook of Computer Science and Engineering</i>, (CRC Press), and as Editor-in-Chief for <i>Handbook of Algorithms and Theory of Computation</i> (CRC Press). He was selected to serve on the Program Committees of various conferences and workshops (including <i>ACM Symposium on Computational Geometry</i>, <i>SIAM Symposium on Discrete Algorithms</i>, <i>Workshop on Algorithms and Data Structures</i>, <i>IEEE Symposium on Parallel and Distributed Processing</i>, <i>IEEE International Parallel Processing Symposium</i>, <i>International Symposium on Algorithms and Computation</i>, and many others). He was Keynote and Invited Speaker at many national and international meetings. </BODY> </HTML> </HTML>
