<head> <title>Purdue CS Alumnus Elected AAAS Fellow</title> <style type="text/css"> <!-- .style1 {font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif} div.figure1 { float: right; width: 30%; border: thin silver solid; margin: 0.5em; padding: 0.5em; } div.figure11 { float: right; width: 35%; border: thin silver solid; margin: 0.3em; padding: 0.3em; } --> </style> </head> <!--#include virtual="/include/header"--> <div align="center"> <table width="750"> <tr> <td><div align="center"> <h1 class="style1">Purdue CS Alumnus Elected AAAS Fellow</h1> </div></td> </tr> <tr> <td height="297"><p class="style1"><img src="images/Hevner2.jpg" width="120" height="160" border="10" align="left" /><a href="http://business.usf.edu/faculty/isds/hevner/">Dr. Alan Hevner</a>, an alumnus of <a href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/">Purdue University’s Department of Computer Science</a> (B.S. '73, M.S. '76, and PhD ’79), was recently elected a Fellow of the <a href="http://www.aaas.org/">American Association for the Advancement of Science</a>, the world's largest general scientific society. Fellows are elected each year whose &quot;efforts on behalf of the advancement of science or its applications are scientifically or socially distinguished.&quot; According to the AAAS Council, Dr. Hevner is specifically being honored for &quot;distinguished research contributions to the field of computer sciences, particularly in the areas of design science research, Cleanroom software engineering, and distributed database systems.&quot;</p> <p class="style1">Dr. Hevner is currently a professor at the <a href="http://www.usf.edu/index.aspx">University of South Florida</a>, where he is an Eminent Scholar in the Information Systems and Decision Sciences Department and holds the Citigroup/Hidden River Chair of Distributed Technology. He joined the faculty of USF in 1994 after serving on the faculties of the <a href="http://www.umd.edu/">University of Maryland</a> and the <a href="http://www1.umn.edu/twincities/index.html">University of Minnesota</a>. He was recognized by Purdue Computer Science as an <a href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/external_relations/alumni/outstanding_alumni/Spring_2007/index.sxhtml">Outstanding Alumnus</a> in the Spring of 2007.</p> <p class="style1">Dr. Hevner’s many noteworthy scholarly achievements include his doctoral research on Query Optimization in Distributed Database Systems and his work with <a href="http://www.computer.org/portal/web/awards/millsbio">Dr. Harlan Mills</a> on pivotal ideas in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cleanroom_software_engineering">Cleanroom software engineering</a>. During the past decade, Dr. Hevner has shown influential leadership in shifting the research paradigm of Information Systems toward <a href="http://desrist.org/">design science</a>, which seeks &quot;to extend the boundaries of human and organizational capabilities by creating new and innovative artifacts.&quot; In 2010, he co-authored (with <a href="http://wfs.cgu.edu/chatters/home/">Dr. Samir Chatterjee</a>) <a href="http://www.springer.com/business+%26+management/business+information+systems/book/978-1-4419-5652-1"><em>Design Research in Information Systems: Theory and Practice</em></a>, which further explores the design science research paradigm.</p> <p class="style1">At the <a href="http://www.aaas.org/meetings/">2012 Annual Meeting of AAAS</a> in Vancouver, British Columbia, Dr. Hevner will be presented with a certificate and rosette, acknowledging his recognition as an AAAS Fellow.</p> <p class="style1">Congratulations to Dr. Hevner on this honor!</p> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>&nbsp;</td> </tr> </table> </div> </body> </html>
