<TR><TD HEIGTH="25"> <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"> <b> Tuesday, September 16<br> 6-8pm<br> RAWL 1086<p><br> </b></font> </TD></TR> <TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT"> <FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"> Mathematica Miniconference: 6pm, Tuesday, September 16 in RAWL 1086, sponsored by the Engineering Computer Network. (RAWL is the new building by the Krannert Building, cater-cornered from the Union Building.)<p> The Mathematica technical computing environment "seamlessly integrates a numeric and symbolic computational engine, graphics system, programming language, documentation system, and advanced connectivity to other applications." Researchers and instructors at Purdue may be especially interested in webMathematica (see below).<p> From 6:00--6:55pm is a totally self-contained miniconference on Mathematica and webMathematica: <ul> <li>"Mathematica Overview", Mark Senn, Engineering Computer Network <ul> <li>presented using Mathematica's SlideShow and Notebook environments <li>compares Maple, Mathematica, and MATLAB languages <li>shows how to do symbolic mathematics, numeric mathematics, and graphics (including ``movies') </ul> <li>"Mathematica at other Universities", Kelvin Mischo, Academic Account Executive, Wolfram Research. <li>"Mathematica at Purdue" (tentative title), Dan Hartley, ITaP Contracts and Licensing Group. <li>"webMathematica Overview", Brendan Elli, Developer of Web Products and webMathematica Product Manager, Wolfram Research. </ul> Lots of free stuff will be given away: books, mugs, travel mugs, tote bags, mouse pads, pens, etc.<p> From 7:00--8:00pm is a more in-depth talk on webMathematica: <ul> <li>"webMathematica: A how-to guide for publishing interactive papers, and deploying calculators, algorithms, and problem solvers over the web". In this session, Brendan Elli, Developer of Web Products at Wolfram Research, Inc. and Product Manager of webMathematica, will introduce this technology and present an in-depth tutorial with live demonstrations of the technology. </ul><p> Kelvin Mischo from Wolfram Research will be available to meet with faculty earlier that day. If you'd like to meet with him, send him email at <a href="mailto: kelvinm@wolfram.com">kelvinm@wolfram.com</a>.<p> Send any questions about the miniconference to <a href="mailto: mark@purdue.edu">mark@purdue.edu</a>. </FONT> </TD></TR> </TABLE> </BODY> </HTML> </HTML>
