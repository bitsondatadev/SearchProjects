<!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="FeatureContent" --> <p><strong><a name="top" id="top"></a></strong><strong>Successful completion of the master's program requires:</strong></p> <ul class="spaced_list"> <li><a href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/academic_programs/courses">10 Three-Credit Courses or 8 with a Thesis</a></li> <li> <a href="#CourseGrades">Course and Grade Requirements</a> <ul> <li style="margin: 0;"><a href="#Non-thesis">Non-Thesis Option</a><br /> <a href="#Thesis">Thesis Option</a></li> </ul> </li> <li><a href="#PlanofStudy">Plan of Study</a></li> <li><a href="#Committee">Advisory Committee</a></li> <li><a href="#ethics">Ethics Requirement</a></li> <li><a href="#communication">Communication Requirement</a><br /> </li> </ul> <p><a name="CourseGrades" id="CourseGrades"></a><strong>Courses and Grade Requirements</strong></p> <p>Up to six semester-hours of credit for graduate courses taken at other institutions may be transferred with the approval of the Graduate Committee and the Graduate School. The grades must be A or B or the equivalent. Application for transfer is made when the <a href="#PlanofStudy">plan of study</a> is submitted for approval. Students may ask the Graduate Committee to accept equivalent graduate courses taken at other institutions in lieu of at most two of the above courses. </p> <p><a href="../current_students/course_transfer.pdf">Instructions on Course Transfer</a></p> <p>Courses used to fulfill the requirements for other degrees (at Purdue or elsewhere) are not eligible for use on master's plans of study, except that courses used for a doctoral degree may be used on a master's plan of study provided the doctoral plan of study does not include any course used for any other master's degree. </p> <blockquote> <p><a name="Non-thesis" id="Non-thesis"></a><strong>For the Non-Thesis Option</strong></p> <p>Three core courses: <a href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/academic_programs/courses/">CS 50200 or 56500, CS 50300 or 53600, and CS 58000</a>. These represent the areas Systems I, Systems II, and Algorithms in the <a href="#AreasTable">Areas and Courses Table</a>.</p> <p>Four other courses from the <a href="#AreasTable">table</a>. These must include courses from at least two areas other than Systems I, Systems II, and Algorithms.</p> <p>Three more Level 5 or 6 courses (not necessarily in Computer Science), at most two of which may be individual study courses. </p> </blockquote> <blockquote> <p><a name="Thesis" id="Thesis"></a><strong>For the</strong> <strong>Thesis Option</strong></p> <p>Three core courses: <a href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/academic_programs/courses/">CS 50200 or 56500, CS 50300 or 53600, and CS 58000</a>. These represent the areas Systems I, Systems II, and Algorithms in the <a href="#AreasTable">Areas and Courses Table</a>.</p> <p>Four other courses from the <a href="#AreasTable">table</a>. These must include courses from at least two areas other than Systems I, Systems II, and Algorithms.</p> <p>One more Level 5 or 6 course (not necessarily in Computer Science), which may <em>not</em> be an individual study course. </p> </blockquote> <p align="center"><strong><a href="#top">back to top</a></strong> </p> <p><a name="PlanofStudy" id="PlanofStudy"></a><strong>Plan of Study</strong></p> <p>Courses used to fulfill degree requirements must be listed on a plan of study and submitted for approval by the Graduate Committee and the Graduate School well before the final session. Grades in the A range (A+, A, A-) or B range are expected, but one or two grades in the C range may be accepted if they are compensated by grades in the A range (regardless of + and -). Other grades are unacceptable. The GPA of the courses on the plan must be at least 3.0. CS 69800, Research. M.S. Thesis, is not listed on the plan of study.</p> <p>Master's programs usually take three or four semesters. The practical maximum load is four courses per semester and two in the summer session. Students with assistantships rarely take more than three courses per semester and one in the summer session. Completing a master's program within twelve months is sometimes possible for well-prepared, industrious students. </p> <p><a href="../current_students/MS_plan.pdf">Instructions for Filing a Plan of Study</a></p> <p><a href="http://www.gradschool.purdue.edu/calendar/deadlineinfo.cfm">Graduation Deadline Calendar</a></p> <p align="center"><strong><a href="#top">back to top</a></strong></p> <p><a name="Committee" id="Committee"></a><strong>Advisory Committee</strong></p> <p>For students in a non-thesis master's program, the advisory committee is determined by the graduate committee.</p> <p>For students in a thesis master's program, the advisory committee consists of the supervisor of the research plus two or more other faculty members agreed upon by the student and the supervisor. Qualified faculty from other departments may serve on the committee but may not form a majority of it.</p> <p align="center"><strong><a href="#top">back to top</a></strong> </p> <p><strong><a name="ethics" id="ethics"></a>Ethics Requirement</strong></p> <p>All MS students that entered after spring 2008 must fulfill an ethics requirement.&nbsp; This is currently to be fulfilled by attending the ethics lecture of the Research Seminar for First-Year Graduate Students once during the first year.&nbsp; The seminar (<a href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/academic_programs/courses/">CS 59100 RS1</a>) is offered only in the fall semester.&nbsp; An announcement of the date, time, and place of the lecture is sent out ahead of time.&nbsp; Attendance is taken at the lecture.&nbsp; MS students cannot register for the seminar: they attend just the one lecture. <br /> </p> <p align="center"><strong><a href="#top">back to top</a></strong> </p> <p><strong><a name="communication" id="communication"></a>Communication Requirement</strong></p> <p>All MS students that entered after spring 2008 must demonstrate effectiveness in communication. For students using the thesis option, this will be assessed in the normal course of their program.</p> <p><br /> For students using the non-thesis option, if the assessment cannot be made on the basis of presentations and papers in courses, the student must write a technical essay at the beginning of the final semester. The chair of the Graduate Committee evaluates the essay. A research paper may also be used if the student is the sole author.<br /> </p> <p align="center"><strong><a href="#top">back to top</a></strong> </p> <p><a name="Entering" id="Entering"></a><strong>Changes in Requirements</strong></p> <p>These requirements apply to all students entering or reentering the Department of Computer Sciences at West Lafayette (&quot;the Department&quot;) as degree-seeking graduate students in the summer session of 2001 or later. <a href="previous/ms_2001.sxhtml">2000 master's degree requirements</a></p> <p>Students are governed by the degree requirements in effect when they enter the Department as degree-seeking students. Students who wish to take advantage of subsequent changes may apply to the Graduate Committee to be governed by <em>all degree requirements</em> in effect at a specified subsequent time. Choosing features from different sets of requirements is not permitted.</p> <p>For students re-entering, the date of the most recent re-entry determines the degree requirements.</p> <p><em>The above requirements for the master's program may change without notice.</em></p> <p align="center"><strong><a href="#top">back to top</a></strong></p> <p style="margin-top: 2em;"><a name="AreasTable"
