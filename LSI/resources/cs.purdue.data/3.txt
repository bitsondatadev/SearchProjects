Organized by outgoing president Chris Nuland and incoming president Javid Habibi, the event took place from 2:00 p.m. to 5:00 p.m. in the Commons of the Lawson Computer Science Building. It was well-attended, as it coincided with Purdue Computer Science's Fall 2011 <a href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/news/EmployeeAppreciationFlyer.pdf">Employee Appreciation Social</a>. Purdue ACM contributed cookies and other goodies to the social.</p> <p class="style1">In addition to displaying several posters of current ACM projects, the special interest groups also demonstrated their work. <a href="http://acm.cs.purdue.edu/sigbots/sighome.html">SIGBOTs</a> (recently returned from an <a href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/news/11-16-11_SIGBOTs.html">award-winning showing at the VEX Robotics Competition</a>) set up a robotics field, and SIGGD showed off a new game design on the video wall.<img src="images/ACM (2).JPG" width="100" height="75" border="5" align="right" /></p> <p class="style1"><img src="images/ACM (1).JPG" width="75" height="100" border="5" align="left" />After sharing their Fall 2011 projects with the Purdue CS community, ACM now looks forward to Spring 2012. In coming weeks, they will publish a newsletter, sharng their plans for the upcoming semester with the Department, corporate partners, and all others interested.</p> <p class="style1"></p> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>&nbsp;</td> </tr> </table> </div> </body> </html>
