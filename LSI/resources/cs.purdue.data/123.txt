<div id="wrappercontentarea"><!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Content" -->The Department of Computer Science at Purdue offers a Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Science. Students from other majors and colleges can also minor in computer science, and many computer science majors choose to double major. Click on the links below to see more about the different degree options in computer science. <p><a href="#Major">Major in Computer Science</a> <br /> <a href="#Minor">Minor in Computer Science </a><br /> <a href="#Double">Double Majors</a> </p> <p><strong><a name="Major" id="Major"></a>Major in Computer Science (Bachelor of Science) </strong></p> <p>Note: The curriculum below is effective Fall 2010 for any incoming Computer Science student. Current students may consider switching to this curriculum as well. See your advisor.</p> <p><strong>Computer Science Courses:</strong></p> <p>The first six (core) courses, which are taken in the freshman and sophomore years, lay a strong foundation for computer science majors. Each student then selects a Track in which to complete 6 advanced courses. Each Track has 2-3 Required Courses and a list of potential electives. A student may complete more than one Track if desired.</p> <p>The flexibility of the Computer Science curriculum comes from requiring only six foundational (core) courses followed by one or more Tracks, which allow students to deepen their understanding in a specific area of computer science. Some intersection between tracks allows specialization in multiple areas, for example, Systems Programming and Software Engineering.</p> <p>All beginning computer science majors are strongly urged to take <a href="https://esa-oas-prod-wl.itap.purdue.edu/prod/bzwsrch.p_catalog_detail?subject=CS&amp;term=CURRENT&amp;cnbr=19100">CS 19100</a>, Freshman Resources Seminar. This is a 1 credit course Computer Science students generally take their first semester. Students are equally encouraged to take <a href="https://esa-oas-prod-wl.itap.purdue.edu/prod/bzwsrch.p_catalog_detail?subject=CS&amp;term=CURRENT&amp;cnbr=29100">CS 29100</a>, Sophomore Development Seminar, and <a href="https://esa-oas-prod-wl.itap.purdue.edu/prod/bzwsrch.p_catalog_detail?subject=CS&amp;term=CURRENT&amp;cnbr=39100">CS 39100</a>, Junior Resource Seminar. </p> <p>Core requirements href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/academic_programs/undergraduate/curriculum/bachelor/track_MI.sxhtml">Machine Intelligence Track:</a></strong> Prepare students to understand, and effectively apply in href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/academic_programs/undergraduate/curriculum/bachelor/track_pl.sxhtml">Programming Language Track:</a></strong> Prepare students to work in fields related to program Track:</a></strong> Prepare students to design and develop secure software and assess systems for secure operation. </p> <p><strong><a href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/academic_programs/undergraduate/curriculum/bachelor/track_softengr.sxhtml">Software Engineering Track:</a></strong> Prepare students to design and develop large software products, be familiar with analysis, design, testing, and maintenance, and work in teams.</p> <p><strong><a href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/academic_programs/undergraduate/curriculum/bachelor/track_systems.sxhtml">Systems Programming Track:</a></strong> Prepare students to build low-level operating system software, system tools, and programs that communicate over a network.</p> <p><strong>Mathematics Courses: 7-8 credits</strong></p> <p>In addition to the College of Science degree requirements (<a
