</span> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="FeatureContent" --> <p>Admitted applicants to the CS graduate program will automatically be considered by the CS admissions committee for <a href="fellowships.sxhtml">fellowships</a> and <a href="assistantships.sxhtml">teaching assistantships</a>. CS faculty members may, at their discretion, award <a href="assistantships.sxhtml">research assistantships</a>. Students with potential for obtaining a doctoral degree are given priority. It is possible to be admitted without support. </p> <p>In the fall of 2011, approximately 44% of the incoming class is supported by departmental funds in terms of fellowships and assistantships. However, approximately 78% of CS PhD students passed their second year receive support from the CS department: about 60% research assistantships, 18% teaching assistantships. </p> <p>All incoming and current CS graduate students may seek support from other Purdue departments and offices. About 8% receive support from other Purdue entities, and about 3% receive outside fellowships. </p> <p><strong>CS Teaching Assistantships:</strong><br /> Current (not incoming) graduate students who want to be considered for TAships should send a request to: </p> <p> <a href="https://nemo.cs.purdue.edu/wjg/pf.htm">wjg@cs.purdue.edu</a></p> <p> Resumes and other relevant material that the student may wish to furnish should be given to the graduate secretary to place in the student's file. Please do not ask the instructors of courses for TAships because the instructors do not assign TAs. The assistant to the head makes the selection.</p> <p><strong>CS Research Assistantships</strong><strong>:</strong></p> <p>Please do not send unsolicited messages. Incoming students will be contacted by faculty members interested in extending an offer based on their application. Current graduate students seeking a research assistantship should attend research group meetings. </p> <p><br /> <strong>Other Purdue Departments and Offices:</strong><br /> The CS graduate office will send out notices to CS graduate students regarding graduate assistantships in other departments.</p> <p><br /> CS graduate students may also contact the <a href="http://www.math.purdue.edu/jobs/ta">Mathematics Department</a> and the <a href="http://www.cla.purdue.edu/fll">Department of Foreign Languages and Literatures</a> regarding teaching assistantships. </p> <p><br /> Positions with <a href="http://www.itap.purdue.edu">Information Technology at Purdue </a>are occasionally posted by supervisors on SSINFO or the ITaP student employment website: <a href="https://www.itap.purdue.edu/hr/employment">www.itap.purdue.edu/hr/employment</a></p> <p><br /> The Libraries Research Department may have openings as well. Please contact the Libraries Human Resources (494-2899) or the hiring supervisor: Michael Witt, Assistant Professor of Library Science, (765) 494-8703, <a href="mailto:mwitt@purdue.edu">mwitt@purdue.edu</a></p> <p><br /> <strong>Residence Hall Assistants:</strong><br /> All CS graduate students may apply for a residence hall assistanship. University residences cover tuition and fees for out-of-state graduate residence hall assistants as though they are an Indiana resident. Applications should be made to the Director of Residential Life, Smalley Center, Purdue University, West Lafayette, IN 47907 in the fall.</p> <p><em>All University fees and tuition are subject to change without notice</em>.</p> <!-- InstanceEndEditable --> <div style="height: 15px; width: 10px;"></div> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceEndRepeat --> <p>Contact Us: <a href="mailto:grad-info@cs.purdue.edu">grad-info@cs.purdue.edu</a><br /> <span style="color: #777">Last Updated: September 06, 2011 03:35PM</span></p> <div style="height: 15px; width: 10px;"></div> </div> <div id="info"> <!-- InstanceBeginRepeat name="InfoBoxes" --><!-- InstanceBeginRepeatEntry --> <div> <span class="header"> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Info Title" --> Useful Links <!-- InstanceEndEditable --> </span> <!-- InstanceBeginRepeat name="InfoLinks" --><!-- InstanceBeginRepeatEntry --> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Link Text" --> <a href="assistantships.sxhtml">Assistantships</a><!-- InstanceEndEditable --> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceBeginRepeatEntry --> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Link Text" --> <a href="fellowships.sxhtml">Fellowships</a><!-- InstanceEndEditable --> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceBeginRepeatEntry --> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Link Text" --> <a href="http://www.gradschool.purdue.edu/students/insurance.cfm">Medical Insurance</a> <!-- InstanceEndEditable --> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceBeginRepeatEntry --> <!-- InstanceBeginEditable name="Link Text" --> <a href="http://www.purdue.edu/bursar">Tuition and fees</a> <!-- InstanceEndEditable --> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceEndRepeat --> </div> <!-- InstanceEndRepeatEntry --><!-- InstanceEndRepeat --> </div> </div> <div style="clear: both;"></div> <div id="footer"> <div style="font-size: 80%;font-weight: 800;float: left;"> Computer Sciences, 305 N.
