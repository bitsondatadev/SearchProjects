Hours: 3<P> Catalog Description: Computational geometry studies how to compute with and reason about geometric objects. The subject is playing an increasingly important role in computer graphics, game software, geometric modeling, geographic information systems, and many other applications. Course topics include convex hull, segment manipulations, triangulations, range searching, Voronoi diagrams, window queries, Delaunay triangulation, duality. Some key algorithms are implemented. Questions of floating-point accuracy and robust algorithm design are considered throughout the course.<P> --> List of Topics (By Week):<P> 1. Convex hull of points. Efficiency considerations. Linear programming and geometric applications.<P> 6. Range search in one and two dimensions. kd-trees, range trees, interval trees.<P> 7. Range search continued; fractional cascading.<P> 8. Voronoi diagram construction; applications.<P> 9. Voronoi structures for higher geometric primitives; medial axis. Applications in CAD.<P> 10. Delaunay triangulation and key properties. Applications in finite element meshing.<P> 11. Arrangements and duality; applications to anti-aliasing. Other transformations with applications.<P> 12. BSP trees; applications in virtual reality. Conversion from boundary representation to constructive solid geometry.<P> 13. Segment and window queries; box intersection. Applications in collision detection and solid modeling.<P> 14.
