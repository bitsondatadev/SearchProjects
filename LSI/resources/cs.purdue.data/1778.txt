Philippe Flajolet, Course at MSRI, Berkeley, June 2004</TITLE> <H1><font color="0000FF">ANALYTIC COMBINATORICS &#038; ALGORITHMS.<br> Philippe Flajolet, Course at UC Berkeley, June 2004</FONT> </H1> <p> Welcome to these pages! We are talking here of the Summer Graduate Programme of <A HREF="http://www.msri.org/">MSRI</A>, specifically the two weeks course: <UL><LI> <b><A HREF="http://www.msri.org/calendar/programs/ProgramInfo/124/show_program">Analysis of Algorithms</A></b> <br> June 2, 2004 to June 11, 2004 <br> <em>Organized by: P. Flajolet, G. Seroussi, W. Szpankowski, and M. Weinberger</em> </UL> </UL> <P> <hr> <P> <UL><LI> <b>Principles of Analytic Combinatorics</b> <br>Set of 10 lectures by <A HREF="http://algo.inria.fr/flajolet">Philippe Flajolet</A> <br> <b>Abstract:</b> The goal of this set of lectures is the precise quantification of properties of large random structures. These include the basic combinatorial types of words, trees, graphs, permutations, and mappings. Problems may be approached from different angles, like probability theory and stochastic processes, dynamical systems theory, or even statistical physics. In this set of lectures, we shall provide an introduction to the combinatorial and enumerative approach based on generating functions, as well as to asymptotic methods relying on (elementary) complex analysis. This chain is what has come to be known under the name of <em>Analytic Combinatorics</em>. As we shall see, thanks to analytic combinatorics, major characteristics of the basic types can be quantified in a systematic manner. Applications to the analysis of algorithms involving hashing, search trees, symbolic manipulation, and pattern-matching will also be outlined. </LI> </UL> <h2>Plan of the course</h2> Will be adjusted on-line depending... The topics will include: <p> <ul><em> 1. Ordinary generating functions &#038 unlabelled structures <br> 2. Exponential generating functions &#038 labelled structures <br> 3. Bivariate generating functions &#038 probability distributions <br> 4. Complex analysis, rational and meromorphic asymptotics <br> 5. Applications of rational and meromorphic asymptotics <br> 6. Singularity analysis <br> 7. Applications of singularity analysis <br> 8. Parameters and limit laws </em></ul> <h2>Context and other lecturers</h2> The director of the course is <A HREF="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/people/spa">Wojtek Szpankowski</A>, and there will be 10 hour sets of lectures given by him as well as by G. Seroussi and M. Weinberger. The other parts of the course should thus be expected to focus on topics related to data compression and information theory.
