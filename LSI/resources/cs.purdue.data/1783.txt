<html> <Title>Pattern Matching Based Data Compression</title> <BODY BGCOLOR="#ffffff"> <LEFTMARGIN=0> <MARGIN=0> <font face="Helvetica"> <h1> <FONT SIZE="5"><P ALIGN="RIGHT"> COMPRIME:<br> A Toolkit for Data Compression Using Approximate Pattern Matching </font> </h1> <strong> <FONT SIZE="2"><P ALIGN="RIGHT"> Personnel: <a href = "http://www.cs.purdue.edu/faculty/spa.html"> Wojciech Szpankowski</a>, Marc Alzina, <a href = "http://www.cs.purdue.edu/faculty/ayg.html"> Ananth Grama</a> </strong> <br> <br> </font> <FONT SIZE="3"><P ALIGN="JUSTIFY"> This research addresses important old and new problems in data compression, in the light of results springing from new probabilistic analyses of digital trees and their generalizations, and (approximate) pattern matching algorithms. We use our expertise in analysis and design of algorithms on strings to bring new ideas to bear on data compression problems. Our goal is not only to gain insight into the usefulness of existing schemes for data compression, but also to develop new algorithms for lossless off-line data compression and lossy data compression (on-line and off-line). We point out that to predict dynamic performance of a data compression method (particularly, off-line schemes) one needs information about second-order properties (e.g., the large deviations and limiting distribution of number of phrases in the Lempel-Ziv parsing algorithm, etc.) which we plan to explore during the course of this research. </font> <h3>Recent Research Results: </h3> This project has led to the development of a variety of theoretical as well as applied techniques for data compression based on approximate pattern matching. A toolkit for compression of still images as well as video streams was developed. This toolkit uses a k-d tree for determining best approximate pattern matches using an existing database. In the case of still images, this database consists of the part of the image that has been coded thus far. In the case of video streams, the database corresponds to an image in the past. <h4>Compression Results for Still Images</h4> A summary of the performance of Comprime and its comparison to JPEG is as
