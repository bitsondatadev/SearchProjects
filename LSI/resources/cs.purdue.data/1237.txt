These are transmissions hidden in plain sight, operating statically as objects or operating dynamically as processes. <I>SPOTs</I> are generated by <I>stego processes</I>, which are application-related <I>cover processes</I> bound to secret-key <I>stegosystems</I>. Any such cover process that can be used to support a stego process becomes a <I>covert channel</I>. This project investigates <I>dynamic covert channels</I> with a focus on generation, detection and testbed evaluation. The application areas are diverse, ranging from finance and numerics to patterns and music.<P> </DD> <DL> <DT><H2><IMG ALT="o " SRC="pics/golddot.gif" width=20px;> NSF CNS-0716398: Dynamic Covert Channels</H2> </DT> <LI> Tasks: Generation and Detection <LI> Locale: From Noise-level to Semantic-level <LI> Application/examples: Series Data, Textures, Decision Trees; synthetic-data & procedure-based generation <LI> Methodology: Theory and Experimentation <BR> </DD> </DL> </DD> <LI><A HREF="http://web.ics.purdue.edu/~pritchey/encoding_examples.php"><B>Examples of Encoded Documents</B></A> </DD> </DL> </div> <div id="pubs"> <!==============================================================> <DL> <DT><H2><IMG ALT="o " SRC="pics/golddot.gif" width=20px;> Researchers</H2> </DT> <LI>Dr. Jorge R.
