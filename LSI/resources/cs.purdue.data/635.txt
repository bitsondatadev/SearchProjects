<head> <title>Elmagarmid, ACM Distinguished Scientist</title> <style type="text/css"> <!-- .style1 {font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif} .style3 {font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-weight: bold; } --> </style> </head> <!--#include virtual="/include/header"--> <table width="750"> <tr> <td><div align="center"> <h1 class="style1">Elmagarmid, ACM Distinguished Scientist</h1> </div></td> </tr> <tr> <td><div align="center"></div></td> </tr> <tr> <td><p class="style1 style1"><img src="images/09ake.jpg" alt="Ahmed Elmagarmid" width="250" height="357" hspace="10" vspace="10" align="left" /><a href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/people/faculty/ake/">Professor Ahmed Elmagarmid</a> is a 2009 recipient of an <a href="http://awards.acm.org/homepage.cfm?awd=157" target="_blank">ACM Distinguished Membership</a>. This honor is bestowed upon members who have made significant contributions to the computing industry and have had at least 15 years of professional experience. Elmagarmid was recognized as a <a href="http://plone.acm.org/membership/distinguished" target="_blank">Distinguished Scientist</a> along with 58 other scientists. There were also 9 Distinguished Educators named, and 17 Distinguished Engineers. With this prestigious distinction members will become part of the <a href="http://awards.acm.org/html/amg_call.cfm">ACM Advanced Member Grades Program</a>. They will also receive a certificate, a pin, and an ACM membership card annotating their distinction.</p> <p class="style1 style1">Elmagarmid is a Professor of Computer Science and the Director of the <a href="http://www.cs.purdue.edu/icds/" target="_blank">Indiana Center for Database Systems</a> and the the <a href="http://www.purdue.edu/dp/cyber/" target="_blank">Cyber Center in Discovery Park</a>. He joined Purdue in 1988, the same year he was awarded a Presidential Young Investigator Award. Elmagarmid's research interests range on a large spectrum of foundational and application-oriented database research. He is also interested in using cyber infrastructure for science and engineering discovery, in education and research policy, and in innovation systems in developing countries.</p> <p class="style1 style1">Purdue Computer Science Alumnus, <a href="http://people.cs.vt.edu/~ramakris/" target="_blank">Naren Ramakrishnan</a>, was also named a Distinguished Scientist of the ACM. Ramakrishnan earned his PhD in computer science in 1997 from Purdue. He is a Professor in the Virginia Tech Department of Computer Science.</p> </td> </tr> </table> </body> </html> </html>
